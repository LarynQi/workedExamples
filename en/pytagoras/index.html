Pythagorean theorem

Problem written by Moussa Doumbouya, named after a great ancien greek thinker.

The Pythagorean theorem, named after the ancient Greek thinker, Pythagoras, is a fundamental relation in geometry. It states that in a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the square of the other two sides.

For instance, let's consider a right triangle ABC, with the right angle located at C. According to the Pythagorean theorem:
BC2 = AB2 + AC2
which implies that:
BC = sqrt(AB2 + AC2)   (sqrt means squared root)

Write a program that continually reads in the lengths of the sides AB and AC, and that outputs the length of the hypotenuse (BC). Below are examples of inputs and outputs of the program.

Enter the length of AB: 3
Enter the length of AC: 4
The length of BC (the hypotenuse) is: 5.0

Enter the length of AB: 1
Enter the length of AC: 1
The length of BC (the hypotenuse) is: 1.4142135623730951

Solution

Show Solution 

import math

def main():
	while True:
		# Read the mass in from the user. type is float
		ab = float(input("Enter the length of AB: "))
		ac = float(input("Enter the length of AC: "))
		bc = math.sqrt(ab**2 + ac**2)
		print("The length of BC (the hypotenuse) is: " + str(bc))
		print("")


if __name__ == '__main__':
	main()
