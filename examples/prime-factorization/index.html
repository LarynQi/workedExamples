Prime factorization is the process of decomposing an integer into factors that are prime numbers. The fundamental theorem of arithmetic states that every positive integer has a unique prime factorization. Here are examples of prime factorizations:

  • 14 = 2 x 7
  • 25 = 5 x 5
  • 99 = 3 x 3 x 11
  • 255 = 3 x 5 x 17
  • 1234 = 2 x 617

Write a program that continually reads in an integer and outputs its prime factors. You may want to decompose the problem by creating the following functions:

  • A function that checks if its argument is prime. Note that a prime numer is a number that is not multiple of any number other than 1 and itself.
  • A function that returns the smallest prime factor of its argument. That is, the smallest prime number greater than 1 that evenly divides the argument.
  • A function that shows the prime factors of a number by repeatedly finding its smallest prime factor and repeating the same process on the remaining quotient until the remaining quotient is 1.
