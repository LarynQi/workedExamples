When two numbers a and b are such that their ratio (a/b) is the same as the ratio of their sum to the largest of the two numbers (a+b)/a, they are said to be in the golden ratio.

The golden ratio is then: Ï•  =  (a+b) / a  =  a / b.

The Fibonacci sequence is the sequence is such that the first two elements are 1, and any other element is the sum of the two elements preceeding it.

  • F(0) = 1
  • F(1) = 1
  • F(2) = F(1) + F(0) = 2
  • F(3) = F(2) + F(1) = 3
  • F(4) = F(3) + F(2) = 5
  • F(5) = F(4) + F(3) = 8
  • F(6) = F(5) + F(4) = 13
  • F(7) = F(6) + F(5) = 21
  • ...
  • F(n) = F(n-1) + F(n-2)
The ratio of consecutive Fibonaccy terms 1/1, 2/1, 3/2, 5/3, 8/5, 13/8, 21/13, etc. for larger and larger terms converges towards one number, ... the golden ratio!

Write a program that estimates the golden ratio as the ratio of consecutive Fibonaccy terms. Here is a possible top-down decomposition.

  • A function that displays the golden ratio at position i (in Fibonacci sequence) for i going from 1 to 100
  • A function that computes the golden ratio at position i, F(i) / F(i-1)
  • A function that computes the Fibonaccy term i, F(i)
